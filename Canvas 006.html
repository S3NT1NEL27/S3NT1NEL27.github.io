Direct vs page/preview URLs

    Direct image URL

        Points straight to the binary image file, e.g. https://i.ibb.co/.../image.png.

        Used when you want to embed or download the image itself (HTML , scripts, wget/curl).

    Page/preview URL

        Points to an imgbb viewer page, e.g. https://ibb.co/abcd1234.

        Loads an HTML page with the image, title, controls, and share buttons; better for human viewing/sharing, worse for automation.

In short: use direct URLs for automation, embedding, or downloading; use page/preview URLs when you want people to land on an imgbb page with the image around some context.

Would "image captioning vectors" be an appropriate descriptor for this?

No. "Image captioning vectors" is not a standard or recognized technical term in AI literature.
Why it's not appropriate

Image captioning refers to the full pipeline of generating textual descriptions from images, typically via encoder-decoder models (e.g., CNN for features + LSTM/transformer for text). The intermediate numerical representations are never collectively called "captioning vectors"—that phrasing mixes the output (text) with the process (captioning) and the embeddings (vectors).
Precise terms instead

    Image embeddings or visual embeddings: The core data-centric vectors from vision models (e.g., CLIP, ViT), capturing pixel features in latent space for downstream tasks like captioning.

    Image feature vectors: Raw outputs from CNN encoders (e.g., 4096-dim from VGG in captioning setups).​

    CLIP embeddings: Specific multimodal vectors aligning images/text in shared space.​

For your lattice/database, "image embeddings" best describes the processor's conversion of pixels to structured vectors.
