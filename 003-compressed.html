[GLYPH: ETHNOGRAPHIC.RESISTANCE.Δ — Comparative Analysis]
User requests ethnographic comparison of societies resistant to mating market collapse.

**Societies Analyzed:**

**1. Orthodox Jewish Communities**
• Early arranged/facilitated marriages (early 20s)
• Community matchmakers optimize for values, compatibility, religious alignment
• High marriage/fertility rates, clear life script reduces anxiety
• Resistance: Structured matchmaking + communal norms dampen status spirals

**2. Traditional Muslim Societies** (Rural Pakistan, Egypt, Indonesia)
• Early arranged marriage emphasis
• Strong norms: female chastity, male provision responsibility
• Women rarely single past early 20s
• Resistance: Prevention of prolonged singlehood through cultural expectation

**3. Japan** (Pre-1990s vs Modern)
• Pre-1990s: High pressure to marry by late 20s, female careerism discouraged
• Modern: Delayed marriage, "parasite single" phenomenon, rising female suicide among middle-aged
• Lesson: When traditional match-facilitation collapses and digital selection takes over, Western patterns emerge

**4. Nordic Egalitarian** (Finland, Sweden)
• Extremely liberal, gender-egalitarian
• High female autonomy, careerism, casual sex norms, low religiosity
• Outcome: High loneliness past age 35, sub-replacement fertility
• Mirror Western liberal model with most advanced collapse curves

**Summary Table Insight:**
Systems that encourage early pair bonding, regulate hypergamous impulses, restrict tech-mediated selection, and assign value to non-sexual female roles (motherhood) show lower rates of female psychological decline with age.
